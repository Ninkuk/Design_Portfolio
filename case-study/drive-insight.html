Drive Insight

Drive Insight is a data visualization and analytics platform for vehicle telemetry. As solo founder and designer, I owned the entire brand identity, product design, and go-to-market strategy — end-to-end design across brand systems, user interface, and marketing collateral, showing how intentional design transforms raw data into actionable insights.

Context

Most drivers generate hundreds of data points every time they drive — fuel efficiency, acceleration patterns, maintenance alerts — but consumer vehicles lack a cohesive way to surface this information meaningfully. The market had plenty of OBD2 readers and generic dashboards, but nothing designed specifically to help drivers understand their own driving behavior and vehicle health. Drive Insight was built to close that gap: a mobile app that aggregates car data and presents it through thoughtful design, clear hierarchy, and intentional information architecture. The goal was simple: make the data matter to the person behind the wheel.

Brand System

Drive Insight's brand identity was built around a single visual metaphor: the eye as a symbol of insight and clarity. The logo itself is an eye constructed from a gradient scale — green to red with yellow and intermediate values — directly mirroring the metrics system embedded throughout the app. This scale measures driver behavior across acceleration, braking, turning, and speed, giving users immediate visual feedback on their performance. The color palette centered on Tailwind's slate spectrum: slate-900 as the primary dark background, slate-50 as the complementary light blue, and a warm golden yellow as the accent color. Typography was Inter — chosen for its modern clarity without the genericness of system fonts. Together, these elements created a cohesive visual system that made data feel approachable rather than intimidating.

Product UI

The product UI was designed around progressive disclosure — rather than overwhelming users with raw data on login, the app walks them through a guided setup (garage view, car registration via VIN, Bluetooth pairing) before any recording begins. Once active, the core experience unfolds in layers: a weekly view shows trips at a glance with overall scores; tapping a trip opens detailed statistics (acceleration, braking, speed — mean, median, max, min); drilling deeper reveals the signature feature — a space-time view with an interactive map paired below a speed chart. Long-pressing the chart highlights your real-time position on the route above, creating an intuitive link between temporal data and spatial context. This layered hierarchy transformed telemetry from abstract numbers into a story users could explore and understand. The color gradient system (green to red across acceleration, braking, and turning metrics) provided instant visual feedback at every level, reinforcing the core metaphor: how well are you driving?

Marketing

Drive Insight's marketing strategy centered on two core messages: personal optimization and data autonomy. Social posts emphasized the immediate value — see your driving metrics, understand your habits, improve your performance — using the gradient scale system as the visual anchor across all collateral. Beneath that was a privacy angle rooted in Mozilla Foundation research: major car manufacturers collect extensive telemetry but offer users nothing meaningful in return, instead selling data to insurance companies and third parties. Drive Insight inverted that dynamic, positioning the app as a tool for users to reclaim ownership of their own vehicle data. The marketing visual language mirrored the product: clean, data-forward, with the signature green-to-red gradient system creating instant recognizability across LinkedIn and Instagram.
